The Forgotten Gateways

Jagri

A traditional Uttarakhand practice led by a Jagria who invokes spirits, using rhythmic music, dance, and chanting. Jagri preserves heritage and brings communities together for spiritual guidance.

Jagar blends singing, poetry, and performance to connect people to their ancestors and the divine.

Roop Kund

Roopkund tells a tale of myth and science. Legends say goddess Nanda Devi killed pilgrims with hailstones. Science reveals two groups died in the 9th century from a violent hailstorm.

Discovery at Roopkund highlights the balance between folklore and natural events in Himalayan history.

Khait Parvat

Khait Parvat is the "Land of Fairies", said to be inhabited by mystical beings drawn to vibrant colors and sound. Local tales warn visitors to avoid wearing bright clothes or making noise near the peaks.

The mountain is where nature meets mythology, with stories of hero Jeetu Bagdwal and mysterious sightings.

Patal Bhubneshwar

Patal Bhubneshwar is a legendary cave, said to connect to the underworld and home to Lord Shiva and millions of gods. Its limestone shapes resemble mythological figures and it's believed linked to Mount Kailash.
