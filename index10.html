Traditions

  • Ancient Period – Devbhumi, sages, holy rivers, epics.
  • Medieval Period – Katyuri & Chand dynasties, Panwar kings, Chardham, Mughal resistance.
  • Colonial Period – Gurkhas, British wars, Tehri Garhwal princely state, Nainital & Mussoorie history.
  • Modern Period – Chipko Movement; Uttarakhand state 2000; renamed 2007.

2. Festivals

Harela

Harela marks the onset of monsoon in July. Families sow seeds, grow shoots, and plant saplings, symbolizing prosperity and harmony.

Ghee Sankranti

Ghee Sankranti, or Olgia, celebrates August harvest. People exchange ghee, chapatis, grains, and jaggery to show gratitude for nature's bounty.

Phool Dei

Phool Dei, celebrated in March, involves girls decorating doorsteps with flowers and rice, singing, and receiving sweets, representing harmony with nature.

3. Dances

Langvir Dance

Langvir Nritya, performed by men in Kumaon, features acrobatics on bamboo poles, balanced with drumming and local music.

Pandav Nritya

  • Folk dance-drama, Mahabharata-based.
  • Artists enact epic tales via singing, dancing, and drums.
  • Performed at fairs and religious events, preserving tradition.

4. Food


More Local Foods

  • Bhatt ki Churdkani: Black soybeans curry.
  • Jhangora Kheer: Millet pudding.
  • Singori: Sweet wrapped in leaves.

5. Folk Music

Uttarakhand’s music uses dhol, damau, turri, and ransingha. Jagar and Chholiya are region’s iconic spiritual and martial songs, present in ceremonies and village fairs.

6. Handicrafts

Aipan art (floor paintings with rice paste), ringaal bamboo crafts, woolen weaving (shawls), pottery and wood carving highlight local creativity.

7. Key Places

  • Char Dham: Kedarnath, Badrinath, Gangotri, Yamunotri pilgrimages.
  • Nainital, Mussoorie, Ranikhet: Hill stations.
  • Jim Corbett National Park: Tiger reserve and rich wildlife.
